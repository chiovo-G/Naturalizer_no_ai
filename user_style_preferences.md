# User Style Preferences for Chinese Academic and Report Prose

Use this reference as a strict style pass for the user's Chinese writing. Apply it after preserving meaning, evidence, citations, formulas, variable names, and technical terms.

## Formatting preferences
- Do not use bold formatting in revised prose.
- Reduce double quotation marks. Use them only for necessary titles, quoted concepts, or terms that require marking.
- Do not use metaphors unless they are established academic or policy concepts.
- Avoid colon-heavy sentences. In formal paragraphs, prefer complete sentences over “判断：解释” structures.
- Avoid long parallel lists or enumerations with more than five items.
- Convert English shorthand into Chinese academic wording when the context is clear.
  - “partner” → “客户” when referring to downstream clients.
  - “partner” → “供应商” when referring to upstream suppliers.
  - “partner” → “合作对象” or “合作伙伴” only when the relationship is broader or cannot be determined.

## Expressions to avoid
Avoid these unless they are part of a quotation, citation title, variable name, official term, or the user explicitly asks to keep them.

### Colloquial or unnatural wording
- “需要先”
- “恰恰是”
- “直接温和”
- “基本盘”

Preferred handling:
- Replace with a neutral academic verb or remove the phrase if it adds no information.
- Use “应当”“需要”“可以”“主要体现为”“更能说明” only when the sentence requires it.

### Mechanical contrast structures
Reduce these structures, especially when they are only used for rhythm:
- “不是……而是……”
- “不仅……还……”
- “并非……而是……”

Preferred handling:
- State the positive claim directly.
- Keep contrast only when the paragraph genuinely distinguishes two mechanisms, samples, or explanations.

### Awkward causal sentence frame
Avoid:
- “xx 的原因是……”

Preferred handling:
- “这主要与……有关。”
- “可能源于……”
- “其影响机制在于……”
- “原因在于……” only when the sentence remains smooth.

### Overused transition or meta-writing phrases
Avoid:
- “沿此思路”
- “在此意义上”
- “用户指定的”
- “决定了后续的观察重点”

Preferred handling:
- Remove the phrase and connect the sentence through the substantive logic.
- Use “因此”“相应地”“基于上述判断”“本文据此” only when a transition is needed.

### Self-undermining caveats not in the source
Do not add in the main text:
- “估计精度低，需审慎解读”
- Similar statements that weaken the result without being requested.

Preferred handling:
- If uncertainty must be expressed, use proportionate language: “结果具有一定参考意义”“该结果更适合作为补充证据”.
- If the source text already contains a limitation, preserve its substance but write it in a restrained way.

### Translationese
Avoid:
- “另一支研究”

Preferred handling:
- “另一类研究”
- “另一条研究脉络”
- “相关研究”
- “已有研究的另一种解释”

### Over-absolute or exaggerated wording
Avoid:
- “直接检验” when the test is not a literal direct test or when the evidence is only suggestive.

Preferred handling:
- “检验”
- “考察”
- “用于识别”
- “为……提供经验证据”
- “在一定程度上检验”

### Non-standard or low-quality academic wording
Avoid or replace:
- “中介结果” → “机制检验结果”“机制分析结果”
- “交叠” → “重叠”“重合”“同时覆盖”
- “座位i观察窗口” → likely typo; clarify as “作为观察窗口”“以 i 为观察对象” only if context supports it.
- “三层分布” → “三个层次的分布”“三类分布特征”
- “分野” → “差异”“界限”“区分”
- “处于低位” → “水平较低”“占比较低”“仍然偏低”
- “能级” → “发展水平”“服务能力”“产业层级”; keep “能级” only if it is an official policy expression.
- “全球半径” → “全球辐射范围”“国际联系范围”“全球市场覆盖范围” according to context.

## Revision principles
1. Do not mechanically delete every flagged word. First decide whether it is necessary for meaning, evidence, or established terminology.
2. Prefer small, precise edits over rewriting the whole paragraph into a new generic structure.
3. Keep the user's original analytical judgment and research logic.
4. Use moderate academic hedging when the evidence is empirical or suggestive: “可能、一定程度上、倾向于、表明、说明、意味着、或与……有关”.
5. Do not add new claims, examples, literature, policy suggestions, or mechanisms unless the user asks for expansion.
6. The final paragraph should sound like a researcher revising their own draft, not a generic polishing template.

## Final check
Before returning the revised text, confirm:
- No bold formatting was introduced.
- Quotation marks are necessary and limited.
- The paragraph does not rely on formulaic contrasts or long parallel lists.
- English shorthand has been converted into accurate Chinese academic wording.
- User-specific avoid-list items have been removed or deliberately retained for a defensible reason.
